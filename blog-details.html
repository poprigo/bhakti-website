The Return of Maximalism in Indian Weddings

For the past five years, the wedding industry has been dominated by the 'blush and beige' aesthetic. Minimalist mandaps, ghost chairs, and whisper-soft florals defined luxury. But as we move into a new season of celebration, the pendulum is swinging back. Maximalism—rich, opulent, and unapologetically bold—is returning to the forefront of Indian weddings.

More is More, But Make it Curated

Modern maximalism is not about clutter; it's about intentional abundance. It's about layering textures and deep, saturated colors to create an immersive experience for guests. It is a rejection of the understated in favor of the unforgettable.

"Clients are tired of playing it safe. They want their decor to feel like an embrace—warm, rich, and full of life."

At Bhakti Decor, we are seeing a massive surge in demand for our jewel-tone collections. The deep emeralds, sapphire blues, and ruby reds that define traditional Indian royalty are being reimagined through contemporary paper artistry.

The Role of Paper Installations

How do you achieve a maximalist look without it feeling heavy or dated? The answer lies in the medium. Traditional maximalism relied heavily on dense floral arrangements and heavy drapes. Today, large-scale paper installations offer the same visual impact but with a sense of airiness and modernity.

Our giant honeycomb spheres, dusted with our signature antique gold finish, are being clustered together to create massive overhead canopies that replace traditional floral ceilings. They catch the light beautifully, creating drama without the physical weight.

Structuring the Palette

To pull off this look, we recommend anchoring the space with two deep primary colors—for instance, a midnight blue and forest green—and using metallic gold paper elements as the neutral tie-in. This creates a rich tapestry that feels luxurious rather than chaotic.

As we embrace this shift, we invite you to explore our 'Royal collection' and see how intentional layering can transform your venue into a palace of color.
